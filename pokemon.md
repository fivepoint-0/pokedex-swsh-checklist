Pokemon Sword & Shield – All Pokemon Locations by Habitat
November 15, 2019 by PowerPyx 1 Comment

Pokemon Sword & Shield has 400 Pokemon, this guide shows all Pokemon Locations sorted by area (Habitat). This Pokemon Location List also includes the spawn chance per area.

There is still free-roam after the story so you can go back and catch more Pokemon. What’s new in Pokemon Sword & Shield is that Pokemon only spawn during certain weather. There are also two types of spawns – “Overworld” are the Pokemon you physically see walking around, “Non-Overworld” are the random Pokemon hiding in tall grass (they are hidden until you start a fight with them).

As with other Pokemon games, there are differences between the Sword and Shield version. Below the unique catches are colored-coded (blue for Sword Edition, red for Shield Edition).

The following catchable Pokemon are exclusive to Sword: Nuleaf, Seedot, Swirlix, Scraggy, Sawk, Rufflet, Solrock, Gothita, Braviary, Jangmo-o, Hakamo-o, Deino, Mawile, Hitmonlee
The following catchable Pokemon are exclusive to Shield: Lombre, Lotad, Spritzee, Croagunk, Throh, Vullaby, Lunatone, Solosis, Mandibuzz, Larvitar, Goomy, Hitmonchan

Overview:

Black (uncolored) = Same for both Sword and Shield version (identical in both games)
Blue = Pokemon Sword Exclusive (different from Pokemon Shield)
Red = Pokemon Shield Exclusive (different from Pokemon Sword)
Overworld = Pokemon you see physically walking around
Non-Overworld = “Random” Pokemon that are hiding in grass and you only see them after they start combat with you
» To see all Pokemon ordered by Pokedex refer to All Pokemon Locations by Pokedex.

Useful Tips and Tricks:

Pokemon Sword & Shield – How to Change Weather – this allows you to immediately force any weather condition you want, extremely useful for finding weather-specific Pokemon!
Looking for a specific Pokemon? On Computer press CTRL+F on keyboard to search the page, on mobile use your browser’s built-in word search to find a specific Pokemon or area on the page quickly. If a Pokemon is not listed, it’s an eveolution that cannot be caught, or a legendary that comes automatically from the story.
